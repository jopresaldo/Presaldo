The Sickness

The SARS-CoV-2 virus is the reason for the illnes called COVID-19. COVID-19 is a sickness that has similar symptoms to another known sickness, influenza (or the flu), but both diseases are cuased byb different viruses.

First thing to note are the symptoms of COVID-19. The range of the symptoms ranges from mild to severe illnesses. Symptoms of COVID-19 include:

    Fever or chills
    Cough
    Shortness of Breath and Difficulty Breathing
    Fatigue
    Muscle or body aches
    Headache
    Sore Throat
    Runny or congested nose
    Nausea
    Diarrhea

Once it enters your body, the virus then attacks your respiratory tract. This includes your mouth, nose, throat, and lungs. Your lower airways have more ACE2 receptors, which is where the virus will go, and will attack there deeper than common cold viruses.

The Variants

Like all viruses, the SARS-CoV-2 virus also mutates. A mutation is when the genetic code is suddenly altered. Think of it as when you copy paste something and it came out all wrong. That's a mutation.

A mutation can either strengthen or weakenn a species, which depends on which strand of genes have been altered. These are the most known mutations of the SARS-CoV-2 virus.

    Alpha
    Beta
    Gamma
    Delta
    Omicron

Delta and Omicron are the most well known here, and are currently the most dominant variant in the world. While Delta spreads fast, Omicron is faster.
