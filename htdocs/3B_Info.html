Transmission

Accoriding to the WHO website (2020), there are 3 main ways to transmit the virus.

    Contact and droplet transmission - Transmission this way occur through "direct, indirect, or close contact with infected people through infected secretions such as saliva and respiratory secretions." This means that a person can be infected via someone who has been infected with the virus sneezes, coughs, talks, or sings.
    Airborne - Airborne transmission is defined as the "spread of infectious agent caused by the dissemination of droplet nuclei (aerosols) that remain infectious" even when suspended in the air for a long time.
    Fomite Transmission - These droplets can also stick to surfaces and stay there, which is what fomite means (contaminated surfacees). A viable SARS-CoV-2 virus can be found on surfaces for "periods ranging from hours to days, depending on the ambient environment."
